# AI Adoption Roadmap

## A Strategic Framework for Baptist Health

The healthcare landscape is rapidly evolving, and Baptist Health is positioned to lead the charge in AI adoption across our marketing and communications operations. This document outlines our phased approach to integrating artificial intelligence into daily workflows.

## Executive Summary

Over the past 14 months, Baptist Health Marketing & Communications has tracked AI adoption through three pulse surveys. The results tell a compelling story: adoption has tripled, confidence has doubled, and the team is no longer asking *if* AI belongs in their work — they're asking *how fast* they can go.

### Current State Assessment

Our team of 117 professionals spans content creation, digital marketing, brand strategy, and communications. The adoption curve has shifted dramatically:

| Metric | Survey 1 | Survey 2 | Survey 3 |
|--------|----------|----------|----------|
| Weekly AI Users | 23% | 51% | 78% |
| Confidence Score | 34% | 58% | 71% |
| Own-Pocket Subscribers | 8 | 19 | 31 |
| Daily Users | 12% | 28% | 45% |

### Key Findings

- AI adoption tripled from 23% to 78% weekly usage in just 14 months
- Confidence in AI tools more than doubled from 34% to 71%
- The number of team members paying for AI tools out of pocket nearly quadrupled
- Daily usage grew from 12% to 45%, indicating deep integration into workflows
- Content creation and research are the top two use cases across all three surveys

## Phase 1: Foundation

The first phase focuses on establishing baseline capabilities and removing barriers to entry. Every team member should have access to at least one AI writing assistant and understand the basics of prompt engineering.

> The most important thing we can do right now is lower the barrier to entry. Not everyone needs to be an expert — but everyone needs to feel safe experimenting.

### Priority Actions

- Deploy enterprise AI writing tools to all 117 team members
- Launch a monthly "AI Office Hours" series for hands-on learning
- Create an internal prompt library organized by use case
- Establish clear guidelines for AI use in patient-facing content

## Phase 2: Integration

Phase 2 moves beyond individual tool use into workflow redesign. The goal is to identify the five highest-impact workflows and rebuild them with AI as a core component.

| Workflow | Current Time | AI-Assisted Time | Savings |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| Blog Post Draft | 4 hours | 1.5 hours | 63% |
| Social Media Calendar | 6 hours/week | 2 hours/week | 67% |
| Market Research Brief | 3 days | 4 hours | 83% |
| Campaign Copy Variants | 2 hours | 20 minutes | 83% |
| Competitive Analysis | 2 days | 3 hours | 81% |

### Integration Principles

- Redesign workflows around AI capabilities, don't just bolt AI onto existing processes
- Measure time savings rigorously — track before and after for every redesigned workflow
- Build feedback loops so the team continuously improves their AI techniques
- Maintain human oversight on all patient-facing and regulated content

## Phase 3: Scale

The final phase scales successful patterns across the organization and establishes Baptist Health as a thought leader in healthcare AI adoption.

- Publish our AI adoption playbook as an industry resource
- Present findings at SHSMD and other healthcare marketing conferences
- Build custom AI agents for recurring, high-volume tasks
- Establish an internal AI Center of Excellence within MarCom

## Bottom Line

Baptist Health's MarCom team has proven that AI adoption doesn't require a massive technology overhaul — it requires a culture that encourages experimentation, measures outcomes, and celebrates progress. Our 14-month journey from 23% to 78% adoption is just the beginning.
